Overview

Mass deportation policies have had significant impacts on families, communities, and the broader social fabric. This site aims to shed light on the statistics, real stories, and historical context of mass deportation, empowering you to understand and act.

Statistics & Economic Impact

  • One-time deportation of 13.3 million people would cost at least $315 billion, while long-term deportation would cost $88 billion annually. Over a decade, the cost is estimated at $967.9 billion.
  • The cost exceeds budgets for key national programs such as NASA, child nutrition, and cancer research.
  • Disruption to industries like construction, agriculture, and hospitality.
  • US GDP would shrink by 4.2% to 6.8%, comparable to the Great Recession.
  • Loss of tax contributions: $46.8 billion in federal taxes, $29.3 billion in state and local taxes, and $22.6 billion to Social Security.

Human Impact & Legal Concerns

  • 4 million mixed-status families would be separated, affecting 5.1 million US citizen children, leading to emotional and financial hardship.
  • Trump’s administration deported at least 3 US citizen children (ages 2, 4, and 7); the 2-year-old New Orleans-born toddler was receiving cancer treatment before deportation
  • Judge Terry Doughterty of the Western District of Louisiana stated, “Of course it is illegal and unconstitutional to detain or recommend deportation of a US citizen.” He criticized the government for deporting a citizen without a meaningful process.

International Impact

Since the start of 2025, there have been over 350 deportation flights. On March 15, Trump invoked the 1798 Alien Enemies Act to deport alleged gang members from Venezuela to El Salvador, even if it wasn't their country of origin.
